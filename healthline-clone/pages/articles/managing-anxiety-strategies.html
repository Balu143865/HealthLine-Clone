Managing Anxiety: 10 Effective Strategies

Dr. Lisa Park Clinical Psychologist
January 13, 2024 7 min read

Anxiety is a normal human experience that everyone encounters from time to time. However, when anxiety becomes persistent or interferes with daily life, it's important to have effective strategies to manage it. Here are 10 evidence-based techniques that can help.

Key Takeaways

  • Anxiety is normal but manageable with the right tools
  • Deep breathing activates the body's relaxation response
  • Cognitive reframing helps challenge anxious thoughts
  • Professional help is available and effective

1. Deep Breathing Techniques

Deep breathing is one of the most effective ways to activate your body's relaxation response. The 4-7-8 technique is particularly helpful:

  • Inhale through your nose for 4 counts
  • Hold your breath for 7 counts
  • Exhale slowly through your mouth for 8 counts
  • Repeat 3-4 times

2. Progressive Muscle Relaxation

This technique involves tensing and then relaxing each muscle group in your body. Start with your toes and work your way up to your head, noticing the difference between tension and relaxation.

3. Cognitive Reframing

Anxiety often stems from negative or catastrophic thinking patterns. Challenge these thoughts by asking:

  • What evidence supports this thought?
  • What evidence contradicts it?
  • What would I tell a friend with this thought?
  • What's a more balanced way to view this situation?

4. Regular Exercise

Physical activity reduces stress hormones and stimulates the production of endorphins, which are natural mood elevators. Even a 10-minute walk can help reduce anxiety.

5. Limit Caffeine and Alcohol

Both caffeine and alcohol can worsen anxiety symptoms. Try switching to decaf beverages and limiting alcohol intake, especially during stressful periods.

6. Practice Mindfulness

Mindfulness involves staying present in the moment without judgment. Regular mindfulness practice can help reduce anxiety by preventing you from dwelling on past events or worrying about the future.

7. Maintain a Sleep Routine

Poor sleep can significantly worsen anxiety. Establish a consistent sleep schedule, create a relaxing bedtime routine, and ensure your bedroom is cool, dark, and quiet.

8. Connect with Others

Social support is crucial for mental health. Talk to trusted friends or family members about your feelings, or consider joining a support group.

9. Set Aside "Worry Time"

Rather than letting worries consume your entire day, set aside 15-20 minutes as designated "worry time." When anxious thoughts arise outside this time, remind yourself to address them later.

10. Seek Professional Help

If anxiety is significantly impacting your quality of life, consider working with a mental health professional. Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) is particularly effective for anxiety disorders.

When to Seek Immediate Help

If you experience severe anxiety, panic attacks, or thoughts of self-harm, please reach out to a crisis helpline or mental health professional immediately. Help is available 24/7.

The Bottom Line

Managing anxiety is a skill that can be learned and improved with practice. Start with one or two strategies that resonate with you, and be patient with yourself as you develop these new habits. Remember, seeking help is a sign of strength, not weakness.
