10 Science-Backed Benefits of Green Tea

Dr. Sarah Johnson Registered Dietitian
January 15, 2024 6 min read

Green tea is one of the healthiest beverages you can drink. It's loaded with antioxidants and nutrients that have powerful effects on the body. These include improved brain function, fat loss, a lower risk of cancer, and many other impressive benefits.

Key Takeaways

  • Green tea is rich in antioxidants called catechins that protect cells from damage
  • Regular consumption may improve brain function and reduce anxiety
  • Green tea can boost metabolism and aid in weight management
  • Studies suggest it may lower the risk of certain cancers and heart disease

1. Rich in Antioxidants

Green tea is packed with polyphenols, which are antioxidants that help reduce inflammation and protect cells from damage. The most notable of these is epigallocatechin-3-gallate (EGCG), which has been studied extensively for its health benefits.

These antioxidants can help neutralize harmful free radicals in the body, potentially reducing the risk of chronic diseases and supporting overall health.

2. Improves Brain Function

Green tea contains caffeine, but in smaller amounts than coffee. This provides a milder, more sustained energy boost without the jitters. But it's not just the caffeine—green tea also contains L-theanine, an amino acid that can cross the blood-brain barrier.

L-theanine increases the activity of the inhibitory neurotransmitter GABA, which has anti-anxiety effects. It also increases dopamine and the production of alpha waves in the brain. The combination of caffeine and L-theanine can have synergistic effects, improving brain function.

3. Increases Fat Burning

Green tea has been shown to boost metabolic rate and increase fat burning in the short term. In one study, green tea extract increased fat oxidation by 17% compared to those taking a placebo.

This effect may be particularly beneficial during exercise. Multiple studies show that green tea can boost fat burning, especially when combined with regular physical activity.

4. May Lower Risk of Cancer

Cancer is caused by uncontrolled growth of cells. Oxidative damage contributes to the development of cancer, and antioxidants can have a protective effect. Green tea is an excellent source of powerful antioxidants.

Observational studies have found that green tea drinkers are less likely to develop several types of cancer, including:

  • Breast cancer
  • Prostate cancer
  • Colorectal cancer

However, more high-quality research is needed to confirm these effects.

5. Protects Brain Health

Not only can green tea improve brain function in the short term, it may also protect your brain as you age. The bioactive compounds in green tea can have protective effects on neurons and may reduce the risk of neurodegenerative diseases.

Studies have shown that the catechin compounds in green tea can have various protective effects on neurons in test tubes and animal models, potentially lowering the risk of Alzheimer's and Parkinson's diseases.

6. May Lower Risk of Type 2 Diabetes

Type 2 diabetes involves elevated blood sugar levels, which may be caused by insulin resistance or an inability to produce insulin. Studies show that green tea can improve insulin sensitivity and reduce blood sugar levels.

A review of 7 studies found that green tea drinkers had an 18% lower risk of developing type 2 diabetes.

7. May Reduce Risk of Heart Disease

Heart disease and stroke are the leading causes of death worldwide. Green tea has been shown to improve some of the main risk factors for these diseases, including total cholesterol, LDL cholesterol, and triglycerides.

Green tea also dramatically increases the antioxidant capacity of the blood, which protects the LDL particles from oxidation, which is one part of the pathway toward heart disease.

8. Can Help You Lose Weight

Given that green tea can boost the metabolic rate in the short term, it makes sense that it could help you lose weight. Several studies show that green tea leads to decreased body fat, especially in the abdominal area.

However, some studies don't show a statistically significant increase in weight loss with green tea, so the effects may depend on the individual.

9. May Help You Live Longer

Given that green tea drinkers are at a lower risk of cardiovascular disease and cancer, it makes sense that it could help you live longer. In a study of 40,530 Japanese adults, those who drank the most green tea (5 or more cups per day) were significantly less likely to die during an 11-year period.

10. Easy to Incorporate Into Your Diet

Green tea is easy to prepare and can be enjoyed hot or cold. To get the most benefits, aim for 3-5 cups per day. Avoid adding sugar or milk, as these can reduce the antioxidant benefits.

You can also find green tea extract supplements, though whole green tea is generally considered the best way to get its benefits.

The Bottom Line

Green tea is one of the healthiest beverages you can drink. It's loaded with antioxidants and nutrients that have powerful effects on the body. These include improved brain function, fat loss, a lower risk of cancer, and many other impressive benefits.

To maximize the health benefits, choose high-quality green tea and brew it properly. Avoid adding sugar, and consider drinking it between meals to maximize iron absorption from other foods.
